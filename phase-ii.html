IDEATION
Take the data and jump right in.

After our successful experience prototype and extensive data analysis, it was time for us to start coming up with potential solutions. We knew we were establishing some form of economic model that established an ecosystem of P2P and P2I exchanges. This meant we were beginning the second half of the double diamond design process: Ideation.

Storyboards

Our primary method to begin ideation was a combination of storyboarding and speed dating. By storyboarding we came up with various possible implementations of the CRYPTA cryptocurrency ranging from highly feasible to entertainingly outrageous. The purpose was to see how many ideas we can outline with a story arc.

A proposed tool to help students log their time.

A proposed online market for student made designs.

A proposed ecosystem that produces coin for engagement.

A proposed P2P exchange of coins for services.

A proposed system of microtransactions that happen behind the scenes.

A proposed system to allow students to invest in CMU based startups.

Speed Dating

The second portion of our ideation process was speed dating which involved us taking our storyboards out to the users the system will be designed for. We established a uniform script and incentivized our users to engage with us. Furthermore, we got some valuable feedback from them and that further realized the pros and cons of our ideas.

Analysis

Taking all the notes we had from our users we jotted everything down on the board and used it to inform the narrowing portion of the double diamond design process.

Micro-transaction

We wanted to explore whether we could outline an envrionment where people do not need to monitor every single transaction.
Allowing for rapid automatic transactions invisible to daily life.

Design Hub + Marketspace

When handling any kind of currency there is eventually a need to spend it.
We began to think about a unique online and offline place specifically designed to be used with our cryptocurrency.

Investment

Many students do not get the opportuntiy to try investing. Also many startups do not get the publicity and support they need from normal people.
We explored if it was possible to solve both problems.

Alumni Relationship

One aspect of our design process that has always been on our mind is what happens when you exit the system.
Can you still shape the system? Is there a new system to join?

Value Flows and Preferred Future

Taking the feedback we looked at the existing systems on campus and tracked the flow and journey of a user from beginning to end. We then used those to identify specific areas for improvement and created new journeys that indicated a preferred future with our suggested systems involved.

Concept map of rapid microtransactions

We explored the emotional shifts a user goes through when interacting with our cryptocurrency to handle all the small transactions they make everyday.

Concept map of an online marketplace

We examined both the seller and buyer side of our proposed online marketplace. We mapped P2P online exchanges.

Concept map of startup funding

We examined both the current state of the world in terms of funding startups. We also present a preferred future of how cryptocurrency could help.

Concept map of Blockchain and cryptocurrency

We mapped out the entire marketplace. This presents various items or events that could classify as desireable or nessecary for users.

Concept map of Alumni involvement.

We wanted to understand how Alumni relationships with the University are driven and where we could insert cryptocurrency into the system.
